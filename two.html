We have iphones at the cost of beauty and spontaneity. Our souls have been sapped so we can labor for corporate entities and the liberal hegemon, and generously given the opportunity to purchase a glimpse of what we could wholly grasp via ‘campgrounds’ and ‘museums.'

It is the nature of life that beauty is fleeting. Man is a sex of permformance, of utility. Woman is a sex of aesthetic, her utility being that of the proliferation of our offspring.

How do we react -cope- with our fleeting utility? Can you prepare? Can you safeguard? Perhaps not—eventually, death will capture what was beautiful;’ an eternal game of cat and mouse.

This is an expansion of what BAP is speaking about, we must seek beauty in an age of tediousness. There is beauty in spontaneity, beauty IS spontaneous; it is not calculated, meticulous, predictable; it is in total opposition to the dredges of monotony.

Entropy; everything that is created will be destroyed. There is beauty in women, in art, and in the stars. Eventually, women will die, art will be destroyed and forgotten, stars will explode. Is there beauty in death? In entropy? Would this make Wollstonecraft right? Or is Burke still right?

Are these notions entirely perverse? Burke argues that there is a clear distinction between these rational categories. If there is beauty in death, is the sublime reflected in death as well? This would surely disprove Burke and vindicate Wollstonecraft, or is death an exception entirely?

Mary Dickinson’s memento mori is sure to have answers. There is beauty in her prose, despite her subject concerning itself with death.

Is there overlap in the rational categories? And even if there is overlap, does this negate their distinct natures? Surely there can be overlap while still maintaining two distinct definitions.

Is there beauty in death?

Anti-capitalist attitudes of transcendentalists. Capitalism has reduced us to cogs, to slaving away doing manual labor. Our tasks are tedious. Were not our tasks in agriculture, in the cities producing, even on the frontier surviving, tedious? Is it the nature of our civilizations to be reduced to a simple cog in the machine, condemned to tedium and barred from spontaneity?
